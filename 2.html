Semantic Markup

It is the markup that relates to the meaning of the content. Eg- heading, paragraph, image, header, footer, nav-tag, etc.

Non-Semantic Markup

It is the markup that relates to the meaning of the content. Eg- div, span, etc.


Semantic Tags

  1. Header tag - header of the page
  2. Footer tag - footer of the page
  3. Main-tag - has the main content of the page
  4. Nav-tag - for Navigation of the page
  5. Article Tag
  6. Section Tag - to group together similar content
  7. Aside Tag - for related links or content

This page will have a Header (header-tag) and a Footer (footer-tag) with the Nav-bar (nav-tag) and different sections (section-tag) as well.

HTML Entites (Character Reference)

An HTML entity is a piece of text ('string') that begins with an ampersand(&) and ends with a semicolon(;)

It's used to display reserve characters (which would otherwise be interpreted as HTML code), and invisible characters (like non-breaking spaces).

Can also be used for characters (symbols, emoji) that are difficult ot type with a standard keyboard eg.- heart symbol, sun symbol, etc.

Some exaples are below-

  • a < b
  • a > b
  • p & q
  • p " q "
  • c     d (space between c and d)
